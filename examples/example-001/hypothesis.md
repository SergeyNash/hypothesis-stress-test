# Hypothesis Map

## Unified Hypothesis Summary

The hypothesis proposes manual prioritization of SAST scan queues to accelerate scanning for business-critical applications and reduce production risk.

---

# Confirmed Signals (Roles + Market)

* AppSec engineers experience CRITICAL operational pain from lack of prioritization flexibility
* Risk-based prioritization exists as a recognized market pattern
* Security vs Delivery conflict is confirmed by both roles and market signals
* CISOs care about visibility and security resource allocation

---

# Internal Illusions (Roles ≠ Market)

* Roles believe manual prioritization solves resource allocation
* Market does not confirm scan queue prioritization as a standalone pattern
* Roles assume AppSec has access to business-criticality context
* Market shows that this context is often unavailable

---

# Missed Opportunities (Market ≠ Roles)

* Market trends toward automation and AI-assisted triage
* Developers prefer predictability over dynamic prioritization
* Existing vendors prioritize vulnerabilities, not projects in queue

---

# Key Divergences

* automation is already preferred
